13 /**
14  * \file visitor.h Provides documentation about expression and statement
15  * visitors and modifiers.
16  *
17  * TOP LEVEL CLASSES:
18  * Class name | description
19  * ------------------|-----------------------------
20  * ExpVisitor | (visit expressions)
21  * StmtVisitor | (visit statements)
22  * StmtExpVisitor | (visit expressions in statements)
23  * ExpModifier | (modify expressions)
24  * SimpExpModifier | (simplifying expression modifier)
25  * StmtModifier | (modify expressions in statements; not abstract)
26  * StmtPartModifier | (as above with special case for whole of LHS)
27  *
28  * \note There are separate Visitor and Modifier classes. Visitors are more suited for searching:
29  * they have the capability of stopping the recursion, but can't change the class of a top level
30  * expression. Visitors can also override (prevent) the usual recursing to child objects. Modifiers
31  * always recurse to the end, and the ExpModifiers' visit function returns an Exp* so that the top
32  * level expression can change class (e.g. RefExp to Binary). The accept() functions (in the target
33  * classes) are always the same for all visitors; they encapsulate where the visitable parts of a
34  * Statement or expression are. The visit() functions contain the logic of the
35  * search/modify/whatever. Often only a few visitor functions have to do anything.
36  */
